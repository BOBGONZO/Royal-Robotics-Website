We are a passionate group of innovators, engineers, and problem-solvers competing in FIRST Robotics. Our mission is to design, build, and compete with cutting-edge robots while inspiring the next generation of STEM leaders. Join us on our journey of creativity, teamwork, and excellence!

What is First Robotics?

FIRST Robotics is a globally recognized organization dedicated to inspiring and engaging students who are passionate about technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM). Through a series of hands-on robotics competitions, FIRST provides a unique platform for students to explore their interests in these fields while developing essential skills such as teamwork, problem-solving, and critical thinking. The organization aims to cultivate a new generation of innovators and leaders by encouraging students to pursue their passions in STEM.

What is their Goal?

These competitions bring together students from diverse schools and age groups, fostering collaboration and camaraderie among participants. Teams are challenged to design, build, and program robots to complete a variety of complex tasks and objectives. These challenges not only test the technical skills of the students but also promote creativity and innovation as they devise effective strategies and solutions. By participating in these competitions, students gain invaluable hands-on experience that prepares them for future careers in STEM fields while instilling a sense of confidence and accomplishment.

How it impacts our community

These competitions not only teach valuable technical skills, such as programming, mechanical design, and problem-solving, but also foster teamwork and collaboration, as each team must coordinate efforts to design, build, and program a functioning robot. Beyond the technical aspects, students develop crucial soft skills like leadership, communication, and time management while working under tight deadlines. FIRST Robotics, in particular, provides an incredible learning experience that blends fun and competition, allowing participants to apply STEM concepts in a real-world setting, engage with industry professionals, and build lasting connections with peers who share their passion for innovation.

The sub-teams at Bishop Reding

Bishop Reding First Robotics Team Leads are dedicated and passionate student leaders. Our team consists of:

Business 

Responsible for securing sponsorships, organizing fundraising initiatives, and managing the team’s budget to ensure financial sustainability. This subteam collaborates with industry professionals, applies for grants, and builds relationships with sponsors to support the team's long-term success.

Strategy

Analyzes game rules, opponent tactics, and match dynamics to develop data-driven gameplay strategies. By leveraging scouting reports, simulations, and predictive modeling, this subteam helps optimize robot performance and decision-making during competitions.

Design

Uses advanced CAD software to create, refine, and test robot prototypes. This subteam works closely with mechanical, electrical, and manufacturing teams to ensure the robot’s components are efficient, durable, and competition-ready while considering weight, material strength, and functionality.

Electrical

Wires and integrates motors, sensors, controllers, and other electronic components to ensure seamless communication between hardware and software. This subteam troubleshoots electrical issues, optimizes power distribution, and ensures the robot’s systems operate safely and efficiently.

Manufacturing

Fabricates high-quality robot parts using CNC machines, 3D printers, laser cutters, and hand tools. This subteam works closely with design and mechanical teams to bring CAD models to life, ensuring precision, durability, and structural integrity in every component.

Mechanical

Assembles, maintains, and optimizes the robot’s physical structure, ensuring all moving parts function smoothly and reliably. This subteam specializes in pneumatics, drivetrain systems, and mechanical linkages, making real-time adjustments to improve performance and durability.

Programming

Develops, tests, and refines code to control the robot’s autonomous and teleoperated functions. This subteam works with machine vision, sensor integration, and AI-driven decision-making to improve responsiveness, accuracy, and adaptability in competitive matches.

Imagery/Media

Manages branding, social media, and visual storytelling to showcase the team’s achievements and engage the broader community. This subteam produces promotional materials, maintains the team’s website, creates video content, and ensures a strong and professional team identity.

Safety

Maintains a secure workspace by enforcing safety protocols, conducting training sessions, and ensuring compliance with competition safety standards. This subteam fosters a culture of awareness, implements emergency procedures, and promotes responsible tool handling to prevent accidents and injuries.
